SATB Features

The SATB features in this application refer to the four part-writing style that builds a choir of Soprano, Alto, Tenor, and Bass voice parts. This writing style is commonly studied in post-secondary music programs to teach good principles of voice leading and part-writing for four distinct voices or parts.

There are set of rules and good practices used for denoting how musical pieces in SATB form should be written. For this application, I have decided to focus on the most agreed upon rules to avoid subjectivity, and because different instructors may have different requirements for part-writing.

That being said, it is possible that I have still made some choices for the rules from my experience that may not match what's expected by some instructors or music professionals.

Rules Accounted For

The rules that this application will analyze and report violations for are broken down into subcategories and listed below.

Note: C4 is used as 'middle-C' for this application.

Voice Spacing

Each voice has a maximum interval expected between its neighbour voices as:

  • Soprano-Alto: One octave
  • Alto-Tenor: One octave
  • Tenor-Bass: Two octaves

Voice Movement

In the category of movement errors including reoslution errors, the following errors are checked for:

  • Leading tones must resolve upward to the tonic or down to the dominant
  • Chordal sevenths must resolve down to the third (of the key).
  • Parallel 5ths and 8ves are prohibited between adjacent chords.

Chord Spelling

A group of voices making up a chord will have some validation on the notes used to ensure that the leading tone and the chordal seventh is not doubled.

Note: If less or more than four notes (voices) are used in a chord, this will also be flagged as an error.

Chord Types Allowed

Any chords diatonic to the progression key, or those that act as mixture chords are permitted. Applied dominant chords are also allowed and will be labeled as such. Any other chords considered 'chromatic' to the key will result in an error with the exception of the Neopolitan chord and the augmented sixth chords, (Italian, French, and German).
